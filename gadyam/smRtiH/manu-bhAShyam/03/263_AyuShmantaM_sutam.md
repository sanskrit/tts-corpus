<details><summary>गङ्गानथ-मूलानुवादः</summary>

She brings forth a long-lived son, endowed with fame and intelligence, wealthy, with numerous offspring, good and righteous.—(263)
</details>

<details><summary>मेधातिथिः</summary>

भक्षयित्वा तु तं पिण्डं **सुतं** पुत्रं **सूते** जनयति । **मेधा** ग्रहणशक्तिः, तया **समन्वितं** युक्तम् । सत्वं नाम गुणः सांख्येषु प्रसिद्धः दैर्योत्साहादिद्योत्यस् तद्युक्तम् ॥ ३.२५३ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>गङ्गानथ-भाष्यानुवादः</summary>

Having eaten the said ball, ‘*she brings forth*’—gives birth to—‘*a
son*.’

‘*Inteligence*’—the faculty of grasping things; the child is endowed
with this.

‘*Suttra*,’ ‘goodness,’ is an attribute postulated by the Sāṅkhyas; and
its presence is indicated by firmness, courage, and such other
qualities—(263).
</details>
