<details><summary>गङ्गानथ-मूलानुवादः</summary>

He shall recognise the four kinds of the means for accomplishing the purposes of man; and he shall always diligently and properly carry them into execution; (100)


What has not been gained he shall seek to obtain by means of force; what has been gained he shall save with careful attention; what has been saved he shall augment by adding to it; and what has been augmented he shall bestow on suitable recipients.—(101)
</details>

<details><summary>गङ्गानथ-भाष्यानुवादः</summary>

**(verses 7.100-101)**

The means of accomplishing the purposes of man are of four kinds. That
is, the following four steps should be taken towards that end:—*viz*:
acquiring, saving, augmenting and giving.

The term ‘*artha*’, ‘purpose’, stands for what is helpful; the meaning
therefore is that what is mentioned here is the means for accomplishing
what is helpful for man; these four Shall be always put into practice:
(100-101)
</details>
